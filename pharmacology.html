Pilocarpine

Direct Cholinergic Agonist

Mechanism Diagram

Step 1: Application

Topical administration to Eye

Step 2: Penetration

Diffuses to Iris Sphincter Muscle

Step 3: Receptor Binding

Stimulates M3 Muscarinic Receptors

Step 4: Signal Transduction

Activates Gq Protein -> PLC Enzyme

IP3 DAG

Step 5: Ca++ Release

Intracellular Calcium Surge

Step 6: Miosis (Contraction)

Opens Trabecular Meshwork -> IOP ↓

Clinical Snapshot

THERAPEUTIC USES
  • Glaucoma Treatment: Lowers intraocular pressure in Open-Angle & Angle-Closure Glaucoma.
  • Xerostomia: Treats dry mouth caused by Sjögren’s syndrome.
  • Radiation-Induced Dryness: Stimulates salivary glands after head/neck radiation.
  • Miosis Induction: Constricts pupil rapidly for eye surgeries.
ADVERSE EFFECTS
  • Excessive Secretions: Profuse sweating (diaphoresis) and excessive salivation.
  • Ocular Discomfort: Brow ache, eye pain, and blurred vision.
  • GI Distress: Diarrhea, nausea, and abdominal cramping.
  • Urinary Frequency: Increased urge due to bladder stimulation.

Atropine

Anticholinergic (The Blocker)

Mechanism Diagram

Step 1: Introduction

Atropine administered (IV/IM/Topical)

Step 2: Signal Release

Body releases Acetylcholine (ACh)

ACh Atropine

Step 3: Competition

Both fight for the receptor site

Step 4: Receptor Blockade

Atropine occupies Muscarinic Receptor

Step 5: No Activation

G-Protein Signaling

Parasympathetic signal fails

Step 6: Unchecked Sympathetic Tone

Heart Rate ↑ | Secretions ↓

Clinical Snapshot

THERAPEUTIC USES
  • Bradycardia: Drug of choice for dangerously slow heart rates.
  • Antidote for Poisoning: Lifesaving for organophosphate and nerve gas poisoning.
  • Pre-anesthetic: Dries respiratory secretions to prevent aspiration.
  • Ophthalmology: Dilates pupils (mydriasis) for exams.
ADVERSE EFFECTS
  • Dryness: Severe dry mouth and dry, hot skin.
  • Vision Issues: Blurred vision and photophobia.
  • Urinary Retention: Difficulty passing urine (esp. elderly men).
  • CNS Effects: Confusion, hallucinations ("Mad as a hatter").

Clinical Snapshot

THERAPEUTIC USES
  • Anaphylactic Shock: First-line lifesaving drug for severe allergies.
  • Cardiac Arrest: Used during CPR to restore heart rhythm.
  • Hemorrhage Control: Applied topically to stop bleeding.
  • Local Anesthesia: Prolongs numbness and reduces bleeding.
ADVERSE EFFECTS
  • Cardiovascular Stress: Tachycardia, palpitations, arrhythmias.
  • Hypertension: Sharp rise in BP (risk of hemorrhage).
  • CNS Disturbance: Anxiety, restlessness, and tremors.
  • Tissue Necrosis: If injected incorrectly into digits.

cAMP levels drop

Calcium Channel Activation

Step 6: Final Effects

HR ↓

(Reduced Cardiac Workload)

Clinical Snapshot

THERAPEUTIC USES
  • Hypertension & Angina: Lowers BP and chest pain by reducing heart workload.
  • Migraine Prophylaxis: Taken daily to prevent migraine attacks.
  • Arrhythmias: Controls irregular heartbeats (e.g., Atrial Fibrillation).
  • Performance Anxiety: Controls physical symptoms like tremors.
ADVERSE EFFECTS
  • Bronchospasm: Dangerous airway constriction (Avoid in Asthma).
  • Bradycardia: Excessive slowing of heart rate.
  • Masking Hypoglycemia: Hides warning signs of low blood sugar.
  • Cold Extremities: Cold fingers/toes due to reduced blood flow.
